Control Birth Pills

  • Contain hormones (estrogen and/or progestin) that prevent ovulation
  • Must be taken daily at the same time for best effectiveness
  • Can help regulate periods and reduce menstrual cramps, but don’t protect against STIs

Intrauterine Device (IUD)

  • A small, T-shaped device inserted into the uterus by a healthcare provider
  • Two types: hormonal (releases progestin) and copper (non-hormonal)
  • Provides long-term protection (3–12 years depending on type) and it is reversible

Vaginal Ring

  • A flexible ring inserted into the vagina that releases estrogen and progestin
  • Worn for 3 weeks, then removed for 1 week to allow menstruation
  • Easy to use and discreet, but must be replaced on schedule

Male Condom

  • A thin sheath (latex, polyurethane) worn over the penis during sex
  • Prevents sperm from entering the vagina and protects against STIs
  • Single-use and easily available without prescription

Female Condom

  • A soft, loose-fitting pouch inserted into the vagina before intercourse
  • Prevents sperm from entering the uterus and also provides protection against STIs
  • Can be inserted several hours before sex, but must be replaced after each use

Contraceptive Patch

  • A small adhesive patch that releases estrogen and progestin through the skin
  • Worn on the body for 3 weeks, followed by a 1-week break
  • Convenient weekly use but may cause skin irritation or hormonal side effects

Diaphragm

  • A dome-shaped silicone cup inserted into the vagina to cover the cervix
  • Must be used with spermicide to increase effectiveness.
  • Provides reusable, non-hormonal contraception but does not protect against STIs

Contraceptive Implant

  • A small, flexible rod inserted under the skin of the upper arm
  • Releases progestin continuously to prevent ovulation for up to 3–5 years
  • Very effective and low-maintenance, but requires insertion and removal by a doctor

Contraceptive Injection

  • An injection of the hormone progestin given every 3 months
  • Prevents ovulation and thickens cervical mucus to block sperm
  • Highly effective but may cause irregular bleeding or delayed fertility return after stopping

Stay informed. Stay protected. Stay you.
