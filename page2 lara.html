Male Condom

  1. Easy to get: Available in stores, clinics, schools, or free at health centers, no receipe is needed.
  2. Protects against STIs: It’s one of the only methods that prevents pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections, which teens often worry about.
  3. Contains no hormones: Teens may prefer avoiding side effects or don’t want a method that requires daily or medical involvement.

Control Birth Pills

  1. Highly effective when taken correctly.
  2. Regulates periods, Helps with acne, cramping and heavy bleeding. Many teens start taking the pill for health reasons and get pregnancy protection as a bonus.
  3. Private and reliable, easy to keep discreet and doesn’t interrupt sexual activity.

Withdrawal

  1. Completely free: Has no cost, no appointments, and no supplies required.
  2. Used when no other method is available: Often chosen in the moment if condoms aren’t on hand.
  3. Simple and private: Doesn’t require talking to a doctor or having products that someone might find.
