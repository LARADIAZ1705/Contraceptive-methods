Basal Body Temperature

  • Based on tracking the length of your menstrual cycles for several months.
  • Depeding on these cycle lengths, you estimate when ovulation is likely to happen (usually around the middle of the cycle).
  • You avoid sex on the days when ovulation is predicted, because those are the most fertile days.

  • Involves observing cervical mucus every day.
  • Clear, slippery, or stretchy mucus indicates the body is near ovulation and therefore fertile.
  • Days with dry or sticky mucus are considered less fertile.

  • A woman measures her body temperature each morning before getting out of bed.
  • A small but consistent rise in temperature shows that ovulation has already occurred.
  • The fertile period is the days before the temperature rise.

Be free to choose your own method, it should be tolerable, safe as well as practical.

Do not forget to ask your gynecologist.
